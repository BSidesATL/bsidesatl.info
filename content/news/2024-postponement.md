BSides Atlanta friends, supporters, and sponsors,

With mixed emotions, we are postponing BSides Atlanta 2024, which had been scheduled for Saturday, September 14th, at the KSU Center on the campus of Kennesaw State University, and are actively looking for a new home for the conference.

We have successfully hosted BSides Atlanta at the KSU Center for several years and were looking forward to doing so again this year.  However, due to the circumstances described below, we feel we have no choice but to look for a new home for the conference.

The Department of Information Systems and Security, the Masters of Science in Information Systems and Ph.D. programs, and the Coles College of Business have been tremendous supporters of BSides Atlanta, and we are incredibly grateful for everything they have done to help the event over the years.

The timeline of events is as follows:

* February 16, 2024 (207 days away from the conference) - Andy made a reservation for space at the KSU Center.
* Tuesday, August 6th (35 days away from the conference) - Andy received an email from the Associate Director of Business Development for the Department of Event and Venue Management about the reservation. The gist of that email was that the conference was now being viewed as an external entity and that we would be required to do a number of things that would be financial in nature and unexpected or face cancellation of the reservation.
* Tuesday, August 6th, and Wednesday, 8/7 - Andy had multiple conversations with the Associate Director of Business Development and his department chair. The department chair is a long-time BSides Atlanta supporter and offered to mediate to reach a favorable resolution.
* Wednesday afternoon, 8/7 - Andy sent an email response and included his department chair, also copying the Executive Director of Event and Venue Management.  In this email, he reiterated the history of the conference being successfully hosted at the KSU Center and the positive benefits the conference has brought to the numerous security-focused degree programs, academic departments, colleges, and the university itself.  Andy’s department chair replied and offered to have a call with the Executive Director to understand why the conference was now an issue.

As of 5 p.m. on Tuesday, August 13th (28 days away from the conference), we have no resolution on this matter, no sign that a resolution is on the horizon, and no indication that the Department of Event and Venue Management is interested in finding a resolution.  Thus, we are forced to make the decision to postpone and locate a new home for the conference in the future.
